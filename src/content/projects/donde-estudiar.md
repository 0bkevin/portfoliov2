## Overview

**Dónde Estudiar** is a case study for an education search and comparison platform built around a simple question: **how do you help students choose where to study without overwhelming them?**

The product direction focuses on making school discovery feel clearer, faster, and more trustworthy. Instead of pushing users through a dense directory, the experience highlights the information students usually care about first: program fit, institution differences, and the tradeoffs that matter when narrowing down options.

## The problem

Education platforms often make an already difficult decision harder.

Students usually need to compare multiple institutions, understand different study programs, and move between broad discovery and detailed evaluation. In many products, that process becomes slow because:

- search results are too generic or too crowded
- important program differences are buried
- comparison views are hard to scan, especially on smaller screens
- users lose context while moving between options

The goal of this project was to design a **school comparison experience** that reduces decision fatigue and helps users move from exploration to evaluation with more confidence.

## Product goals

The case study was shaped around a few practical outcomes:

- make it easier to **search schools and study programs**
- create a cleaner way to **compare education options side by side**
- surface the most decision-relevant information earlier
- keep the experience readable on mobile and desktop
- give the interface a more credible, editorial, and trustworthy feel

## What I focused on

### 1. Search that supports real decision-making

The search experience was designed to do more than just return results. The intention was to help users quickly identify which institutions and programs deserved deeper review.

That meant prioritizing:

- clearer result hierarchy
- faster scanning patterns
- less visual noise
- a stronger distinction between browsing and evaluating

### 2. Comparison views that stay readable

A major part of the product direction centered on **comparing schools and programs** without turning the UI into a spreadsheet.

The design leans on:

- structured comparison cards
- selective information density
- visual grouping for key attributes
- layouts that remain usable on mobile screens

### 3. Trust and clarity in the interface

Education choices are high-stakes, so the product tone needed to feel reliable rather than promotional. The interface was intentionally pushed toward a calmer visual system, with enough structure to guide decisions while keeping the experience approachable.

## Outcome

The final direction positions **Dónde Estudiar** as a more useful **education discovery platform** for students who want to compare study options with less friction.

Instead of overwhelming users with too much information too early, the experience creates a smoother path through:

- discovering schools
- reviewing program details
- comparing options
- shortlisting the best fit

The result is a product narrative that feels practical, trustworthy, and better aligned with the real search intent behind queries like:

- best schools for a specific program
- compare universities and colleges
- find where to study
- evaluate education options by fit
